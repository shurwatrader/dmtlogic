As someone over PDT, I took that trade with shares, and scaled out (I will explain this more later). For you trying to build your account, you can take an option to have more leverage with less money. I would look at slightly out of the money (OTM) call options with at least a week or maybe two until expiry so you can hold at least one contract as a multi-day runner. I like to use the same indicators on the chart of the value of the option contract as well. So for the META 482.5 macro level pivot, you would pull up the META 500c for 6/14 expiry because you know you have room to 500+ in the coming days to weeks. Following this macro level option example, META has now confirmed the macro level break and you have identified the contract you want to buy. You pull up the chart of the contract to find a good entry. The contract LOD was 3.35 (or $335 because options control 100 shares). The daily 10 SMA for the option contract price is 3.45, so I would bid 3.45 (which requires $345 buying power). This would have filled just below the LOD (low of day), and then went as high as 10.80 by the next day (or $1080). You would take profit based on the underlying (META going into supply zones) as well as the contract's supply zones, which were the daily 20 SMA and EMAs, and then the upper Bollinger Band on the next day.
