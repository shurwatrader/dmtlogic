dmtdr 06/13/2026 1:30 PM
Now here is an example of waiting for a retracement after a breakout, or a breakdown in this case. On Friday 6/5/26, TSLA lost the daily 20e and 50e. This is showing on a 10m chart with the 5/12e clouds, but the green and blue bands are MTF bands (multi-timeframe analysis), which just means it's pulling levels from a daily chart and overlaying them on a 10m chart so you have two charts in one -- for these charts, the green band is the same as the. daily 20e, and the blue bands are the daily 50e. Each white arrow on this chart represents a valid entry, and for each entry, your risk level is the previous 10m candle high (same can be done on a 5m chart using a previous 5m high or low as risk, but it's a little more aggressive and slightly more likely to get stopped out, but also more likely to get an entry as sometimes the 10m bands are too far away when momentum is very strong). The first entry is the rejection of the daily 20e (green MTF band), the next entry is the bounce back up to the 10m 5/12e cloud, the third entry is the loss of the daily 50e (blue MTF band), the fourth, fifth, or sixth entries would be on a bounce back up into the 10 5/12e cloud. Keep in mind, the further you are from the original breakdown (green or blue band), the more likely it will eventually bounce back up and actually reclaim the red 5/12e cloud, so I wouldn't really take any lower entries than the ones I pointed out. The first few are obviously the best ones, and you could stay in the trade until there's a reclaim of the 5/12e cloud, which doesn't happen until the very end of the day. The arrows could also represent add points if you scaled out of your position and would like to add back along the trend. (edited)
